Designer Application

Design Like Mad will begin accepting applications March 1st. Please check back later.

Applications are due August 29th, midnight. Due to a large volume of applications this year, late submissions will not be accepted.

Design Like Mad gives deserving, local non-profit organizations access to pro-bono design help from professional designers and design students. Last year 60 Design Like Mad designers and developers completed projects for 21 non-profits in 12 hours. Teams included graphic designers, web designers and developers, architects, and interior designers (view last year's projects).

Designers and developers are required to attend a pre-planning meeting on the evening of Wednesday, September 27th (Time TBD), and the event on Saturday, September 27th from 9am - 9pm.

Applications are due July 25th at 5pm.

Design Like Mad gives deserving, local non-profit organizations access to pro-bono design help from professional designers and design students. Last year 60 Design Like Mad designers and developers completed projects for 21 non-profits in 12 hours. Teams included graphic designers, web designers and developers, architects, and interior designers (view last year's projects).

Organizations will be chosen based on their needs, type of project, willingness to implement the final product, and positive impact on the community. Any Madison non-profit is eligible, except for partisan political and religious organizations.

Responsibilities of the Non-Profit Organizer (the point of contact for this project) include:

  • Communicating the goal and scope of the project
  • Writing and submitting copy
  • Gathering any existing imagery and style guide (if available)
  • Compliance with copyright law for all content provided
  • For web projects: obtaining login information for hosting services (and any other web-related login information - ie. Wordpress)
  • For Architecture and Interior Design Projects: providing building plans, floor plans, or any other necessary plans for your project
  • Communicating with their design team before the event
  • Encouraging community members (and members of your organization) to attend the final presentations

Items must be submitted to kate@designlikemad.org by September 1st, 2014 at 5pm.

ADDITIONALLY: It is MANDATORY that the non-profit organizer (or a representative of the organization) is present at the pre-planning meeting on Wednesday, September 17th from 6pm - 9pm and during the designated times. Saturday, September 27th. Designated times include 9am for the start, 1pm for the first round of critiques, 4:00pm for the second round of critiques, and 8pm for the final presentations. Non-profit organizers will not be permitted to stay at the Library outside of the designated times as a courtesy to the designers.

Organizations chosen to participate will be announced on August 4th. Design teams will be announced on September 5th.

By submitting an application to Design Like Mad, you agree to our Talent and Work Release Agreements.
